Summary: UptimeMetricPro does not sell your personal data. We collect limited technical data to operate the site and serve relevant advertising via Google AdSense. You have the right to opt out of personalised advertising at any time.

1. Who We Are

UptimeMetricPro ("we", "us", "our") is a website uptime and service status monitoring tool operated by an individual based in the United Kingdom. We are the data controller for personal data collected through this website.

Our website address is: uptimemetricpro.com

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store and protect your personal data when you visit UptimeMetricPro, and sets out your rights under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

2. What Data We Collect

We collect the following categories of data when you use UptimeMetricPro:

  • Technical data: IP address, browser type and version, operating system, referring URL, pages visited, time and date of visit, and time spent on pages.
  • Usage data: Which status pages you viewed, search queries entered into our tool, and community issue reports you submit.
  • Cookie data: Data stored in cookies placed by our site and by third-party services including Google AdSense and Google Analytics. See Section 4 for full details.
  • Contact form data: If you submit a message via our contact form, we collect your name, email address and the content of your message.

We do not collect any special category data (such as health, financial, or biometric information) and we do not require you to create an account to use this site.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use the data we collect for the following purposes, each supported by a lawful basis under UK GDPR:

  • To operate and improve the site (Legitimate Interest) — Technical and usage data helps us understand how the site is used, diagnose errors and improve performance.
  • To serve advertising (Consent) — We use Google AdSense to display advertisements. Personalised ads are only served where you have given explicit consent via our cookie consent banner.
  • To respond to enquiries (Contract / Legitimate Interest) — If you contact us via our contact form, we use your data solely to respond to your message.
  • To comply with legal obligations (Legal Obligation) — We may retain certain data where required to do so by applicable law.

We do not use your data for automated decision-making or profiling beyond what is described above.

4. Cookies and Tracking

We use cookies — small text files stored on your device — to make our site function correctly and to analyse how it is used. When you first visit UptimeMetricPro, you will be presented with a cookie consent banner. You may accept or decline non-essential cookies at any time.

Essential cookies (no consent required):

  • Cookie consent preference — stores whether you have accepted or declined non-essential cookies.

Analytics cookies (require consent):

  • Google Analytics — collects anonymised usage data to help us understand site traffic and behaviour.

Advertising cookies (require consent):

  • Google AdSense — places cookies to serve relevant advertisements. If you decline advertising cookies, you will see non-personalised ads instead.

You can withdraw your consent at any time by clearing your browser cookies and revisiting the site, which will re-present the consent banner. You can also manage cookie preferences through your browser settings.

5. Advertising (Google AdSense)

UptimeMetricPro uses Google AdSense to display advertisements. Google AdSense is operated by Google LLC. When advertising cookies are accepted, Google may use cookies and device identifiers to show you advertisements based on your prior visits to this website and other websites.

Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visit to this site and other sites on the internet. You may opt out of personalised advertising by visiting Google's Ads Settings.

If you are located in the UK or EEA, Google will only use your data for personalised advertising where you have given consent. Where consent has not been given, you will receive non-personalised advertisements only.

Note: We do not control the content of advertisements displayed by Google AdSense. Google's Privacy Policy governs how Google uses data collected through AdSense and can be found at policies.google.com/privacy.

6. Third-Party Services

UptimeMetricPro uses the following third-party services, each of which may process limited data as described:

  • Google Analytics — web analytics service operated by Google LLC. Data is anonymised where possible and processed in accordance with Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Google AdSense — advertising service operated by Google LLC. See Section 5 above.
  • Google Fonts — font delivery service. Google may log requests for font files including your IP address.
  • Cloudflare Pages — website hosting and content delivery. Cloudflare may process technical data including IP addresses to deliver the site and for security purposes.

This website contains links to and monitoring information about third-party services. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those services. We are not affiliated with, endorsed by, or partnered with any of the services listed on this site.

7. Data Retention

We retain data only for as long as necessary for the purpose for which it was collected:

  • Analytics data — retained for up to 26 months in Google Analytics, after which it is automatically deleted.
  • Contact form submissions — retained for up to 12 months, then securely deleted.
  • Cookie consent records — stored in your browser until you clear your cookies or withdraw consent.

We do not store any payment data. We do not maintain user accounts.

8. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

As a UK resident, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Right of access — you may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification — you may request that we correct inaccurate data we hold about you.
  • Right to erasure — you may request that we delete your personal data where there is no legitimate reason for us to continue processing it.
  • Right to restrict processing — you may request that we restrict how we use your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to object — you may object to processing based on legitimate interests, including for direct marketing purposes.
  • Right to data portability — you may request a copy of data you have provided to us in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent (such as advertising cookies), you may withdraw that consent at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the form on our Contact page. We will respond to all legitimate requests within 30 days as required by UK GDPR.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk if you believe your data has been handled unlawfully.

9. Children's Privacy

UptimeMetricPro is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us via our Contact page and we will delete it promptly.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or in applicable law. Where changes are significant, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

Continued use of UptimeMetricPro following any changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
